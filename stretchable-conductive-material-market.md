<p><p>The stretchable conductive material market includes several key types: graphene, carbon nanotubes, silver, and copper. Graphene offers excellent electrical conductivity and flexibility, making it ideal for wearable electronics. Carbon nanotubes are renowned for their strength and conductivity but can be expensive to produce. Silver, with its superior conductivity, is often used in various applications, though it's less stretchable. Copper is widely utilized for traditional conductors but poses challenges in stretchable applications due to brittleness. Each material serves distinct applications based on their properties.</p></p>
